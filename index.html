01 — Origin

The Blade
Philosophy

The name TSURAGI derives from the Japanese word 剣 (tsurugi) — an ancient straight sword, symbolizing discipline, honor, precision, and deadly efficiency.

Founded in 2029 in Tokyo by former engineers from aerospace, Formula engineering, robotics, and advanced materials research, TSURAGI emerged from a singular vision:

"Modern supercars have become too similar. Too much noise. Too little soul."

European hypercars meant theatricality. American muscle meant brute force. Electric vehicles meant cold efficiency.

TSURAGI creates mechanical weapons of art.

剣 TSURUGI The Disciplined Blade
02 — Philosophy

Core Manifesto

01

Blade Precision

Every automobile is designed as a blade. Not "beautiful form," but form as an engineering instrument.

  • Sharp body geometry
  • Pressure-managed aero
  • Negative space airflow channels
  • Tension-driven surfacing
02

Silent Aggression

TSURAGI doesn't scream. It looks dangerous without cheap visual noise. Luxury meets intimidation.

Lamborghini = theatrical aggression Ferrari = emotional sculpture Porsche = precision engineering TSURAGI = disciplined weapon
03

Human-Centered Technology

Even in 2035, TSURAGI doesn't build "rolling smartphones." The driver is the center of the machine.

  • Tactile controls
  • Haptic metal interfaces
  • AI assist without domination
  • Analog emotional driving feedback
04

Japanese Minimal Brutalism

Inspired by samurai armor, katana geometry, Tokyo architecture, Shinkansen aerodynamics, and origami structural engineering.
